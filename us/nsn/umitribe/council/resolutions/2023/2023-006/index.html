2023-006. Employment Contracts Authorization Act of 2023

WHEREAS; On July 22, 2023, the citizens of the Upper Mattaponi Tribe ("Tribe") voted to adopt a new constitution ("Constitution") to assert the Tribe's sovereign authority as a federally-recognized Indian tribe and to reorganize the Tribe's government to exercise its full authority in a fair and equitable manner; and,

WHEREAS; The Constitution establishes an executive branch of government comprised of the Chief, Assistant Chief, and various Departments and programs; and,

WHEREAS; The Department of Finance, the Department of Records Management, and other key Departments serve critical needs in the operation of the Tribe's government, and employing well-qualified individuals as Directors of these Departments will help ensure the efficient and professional delivery of services; and,

WHEREAS; In order to retain well-qualified individuals to serve in these critical positions, the Tribe must offer enforceable employment contracts in order to attract experienced professionals to work for the Tribe; and,

WHEREAS; The Council has determined that it is in the best interests of the Tribe to authorize the Chief to enter into employment contracts with Directors of key Departments in the Tribe, and to authorize a limited waiver of sovereign immunity to allow for the enforcement of the employment contracts in the Tribe's Judicial Branch of government.

NOW THEREFORE BE IT RESOLVED, that the Council hereby enacts the attached law entitled, "Employment Contracts Authorization Act of 2023".

CERTIFICATION

The foregoing resolution was duly voted upon by the Council on September 23rd, 2023, at a Regular Meeting with a vote of 3 in favor and 1 opposed, Ø abstaining, and 2 absent, pursuant to the authority vested in the Council by the Constitution of the Tribe.

Speaker of the Council

Attachment

Section 1.1. Short Title

This enactment shall be known as, the "The Employment Contracts Authorization Act of 2023" ("Act").

Section 1.2. Purpose

The purpose of this Act is to authorize the Chief to enter into employment contracts with qualified individuals to serve as the Director of Finance and as the Director of Facilities and Infrastructure, which may include a limited waiver of sovereign immunity to allow for the enforcement of the employment contracts in the Tribe's Judicial Branch of government.

Section 1.3. Findings

The Council hereby finds and declares that:

(a) On July 22, 2023, the citizens of the Upper Mattaponi Tribe ("Tribe") voted to adopt a new constitution ("Constitution") to assert the Tribe's sovereign authority as a federally-recognized Indian tribe and to reorganize the Tribe's government to exercise its full authority in a fair and equitable manner; and,

(b) The Constitution establishes an executive branch of government comprised of the Chief, Assistant Chief, and various Departments and programs; and,

(c) The Department of Finance, the Department of Records Management, and other key Departments serve critical needs in the operation of the Tribe's government, and employing well-qualified individuals as Directors of these Departments will help ensure the efficient and professional delivery of services; and,

(d) In order to retain well-qualified individuals to serve in these critical positions, the Tribe must offer enforceable employment contracts in order to attract experienced professionals to work for the Tribe; and,

(e) The Council has determined that it is in the best interests of the Tribe to authorize the Chief to enter into employment contracts with Directors of key Departments in the Tribe, and to authorize a limited waiver of sovereign immunity to allow for the enforcement of the employment contracts in the Tribe's Judicial Branch of government.

Section 1.4. Authorization of Employment Contracts to Key Directors

The Council hereby authorizes the Chief to enter into employment contacts with well- qualified individuals to serve as the Director of the Department of Finance, the Director of the Department of Records Management, and other Directors and professional personnel of key Departments in the Tribe identified by the Chief. In recognition that each Chief elected to serve will desire to employ key policy making, 'cabinet-level', and administrative appointees, all policy-level personnel, key administrative staff, and political appointees shall not be entitled to an employment contract with the Tribe and shall serve at the pleasure of the Chief.

Section 1.5. Limited Waiver of Sovereign Immunity

The Chief shall be authorized to include a limited waiver of sovereign immunity in the employment contracts for the Director of the Department of Finance, the Director of the Department of Records Management, and other Directors of key Departments to allow for the enforcement of the contracts; provided, that such waiver shall be limited exclusively to the payment of up to three years annual salary of the effected individual, and that enforcement of the contracts shall be exclusively in the Judicial Branch of government of the Tribe.
